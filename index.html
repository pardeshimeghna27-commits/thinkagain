5 signs a relationship might not be as healthy as it feels

Sometimes things feel fine because we have adapted to them. These signs are not accusations ‚Äî they are gentle nudges to pay attention.

1

3

4
You feel worse about yourself than you did before this relationship.
5
Your feelings are regularly dismissed, minimised, or laughed at.

Patterns

What is the cycle of tension and repair?

Many harmful dynamics have a recognisable rhythm ‚Äî tension builds, something happens, there is a honeymoon period, and it starts again.

The repair phase can feel so good ‚Äî loving, full of remorse ‚Äî that it resets your sense of reality. Over time, you may find yourself mainly waiting for these good moments. Recognising the cycle is not about blaming anyone; it is about seeing the full picture.

Why it is hard to trust your own memory

When someone consistently questions your recollection of events, it can slowly make you doubt your own mind. This has a name, and it is not your fault.

This experience ‚Äî sometimes called memory questioning or reality-shifting ‚Äî can leave you feeling foggy, overly apologetic, and reliant on the other person to tell you what happened. Keeping a private journal of events can help you reconnect with your own account of things.

What healthy looks like

Disagreements in a healthy relationship

Conflict is normal ‚Äî all couples and families argue. The difference is in how it happens and how it ends.

In a healthy disagreement, both people feel heard even if they do not agree. Neither person uses silence as punishment, threatens to leave, or brings up unrelated past mistakes. Both people feel safe to say how they feel without fear of the reaction.

Growth

Understanding your own patterns

Sometimes the most useful thing is to look at the role we play ‚Äî not with blame, but with curiosity. How do you typically respond when you feel unsafe?

People often develop protective patterns in childhood that no longer serve them in adult relationships ‚Äî things like people-pleasing, emotional shutdown, or over-explaining. Recognising these with compassion is the beginning of real change.

FAQ

Questions people
are afraid to ask

No question is too small or too embarrassing. These are the ones we hear most often.

Is it normal to feel more anxious around someone you love?
Love can sometimes feel complicated, but ongoing anxiety around a specific person ‚Äî especially if it is about how they will react ‚Äî is worth paying attention to. Feeling relaxed and safe with someone you love is not an unrealistic expectation. It is actually what closeness should feel like.
What if they are kind most of the time?
This is one of the most common and painful parts of difficult relationships. The good times are real ‚Äî and they make everything else more confusing. The question is not whether someone is kind sometimes, but whether the unkind moments are causing you real pain, and whether the pattern is changing over time.
What if I am the one causing harm?
Asking this question already says something important about you. Many people who behave harmfully have no idea ‚Äî the fact that you are asking means you are already doing something they often cannot. Speaking to a counsellor is one of the most courageous and effective things you can do if you want to change patterns you do not like in yourself.
Why do I keep going back even when I know it is not right?
This is incredibly common and has nothing to do with weakness or lack of intelligence. Attachment, hope, financial ties, fear, and even neurological patterns can make leaving feel impossible. Please do not judge yourself for this. Reaching out ‚Äî like you are doing now ‚Äî is already part of the process.
My friend will not listen to me. What do I do?
This is heartbreaking. People often need to reach their own conclusions in their own time ‚Äî and pushing too hard can sometimes cause them to defend the relationship and push you away. The most powerful thing you can do is stay present, keep the door open, and let them know you are there without judgment ‚Äî no matter what they decide.
How do I talk to someone about my concerns without making them defensive?
Lead with curiosity instead of conclusions. Instead of "I think your partner is controlling you", try "I have noticed you seem stressed lately ‚Äî how are you feeling in your relationship?" Sharing observations about how your friend seems, rather than what you think about their partner, tends to open conversations rather than close them.
Is ThinkAgain a crisis service?
No ‚Äî ThinkAgain is a reflection and awareness tool, not a clinical or crisis service. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please contact emergency services (999 in the UK, 911 in the US). For ongoing support, the Resources section has links to trained helplines and counselling services.
When should I speak to a professional?
Any time you feel ready ‚Äî you do not need to be in crisis to benefit from talking to a counsellor. If you are feeling consistently anxious, confused, or unsafe in a relationship; if you want to understand your own patterns; or if you are supporting someone going through something difficult ‚Äî these are all good reasons to reach out. The Resources section has options for every budget.
